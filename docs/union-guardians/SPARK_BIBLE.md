# UNION GUARDIANS - SPARK BIBLE
Version: 1.0
Status: Official AAA franchise system specification
Owner: Creative Direction - Union Guardians
Phase: 5 - Collectible Creature Ecosystem

## Purpose
This document defines the official collectible creature system for Union Guardians as a long-term franchise pillar.

This system is not a copy of any existing monster-collection franchise. Its identity is based on Union Guardians values: discipline, perseverance, respect, and purposeful growth.

---

## 1. Official System Name

### 1.1 Name candidates (original)
1. Sparks
2. Lumens
3. Sigils
4. Echoes
5. Emberlings
6. Vinctors
7. Nodos Vivos
8. Virtus

### 1.2 Evaluation criteria
- Child-friendly pronunciation
- Global brand potential
- Merchandising strength

- Scalability for 500+ entries
- Distinctiveness from other franchises

### 1.3 Selected official name

### 1.4 Why Sparks is the best choice
- Universal and easy to remember in multiple languages.
- Fits the emotional fantasy of "a small force that ignites something greater".
- Works for all elements, not only fire.
- Strong for products: cards, stickers, badges, apparel, toys.
- Supports future brand architecture: Spark Core, Spark Events, Spark Atlas, Spark Trials.

Official sentence:
A Spark is a living catalyst that amplifies a Guardian's path.

## 2. What Sparks Are and Their Origin
Sparks are micro-entities of living resonance born from the intersection of three forces:
- Elemental current of the world
- Echo of trained virtue
- Regional memory of the land

They are not pets and not weapons. They are resonance organisms that respond to behavior, consistency, and intent.

### 2.1 Birth model
Sparks emerge at Resonance Nodes, places where training history and elemental pressure overlap.

Birth phases:
1. Ambient resonance accumulation
2. Virtue imprint adhesion
3. Core pulse stabilization
4. Identity crystallization
5. First binding opportunity

### 2.2 Narrative identity
If Guardians are oath-bearers, Sparks are path-shapers.
A Guardian embodies a value.
A Spark modifies how that value manifests in action.

---

## 3. Relationship with Guardians
A Guardian can bond with Sparks through Attunement Slots.

### 3.1 Bond logic
- Sparks do not replace Guardians.
- Sparks do not command Guardians.
- Sparks amplify style, rhythm, or strategy.

### 3.2 Bond layers
- Tactical bond: modifies combat behavior
- Emotional bond: modifies progression tone
- Legacy bond: unlocks lore and cosmetic signatures

### 3.3 Slot framework (base for future balancing)
- Early game: 1 Spark slot
- Mid game: 2 Spark slots
- Endgame mastery: 3 Spark slots (with restrictions)

---

## 4. Rarity System
Official rarity ladder:
1. Common
2. Rare
3. Epic
4. Legendary
5. Mythic
6. Ascendant (event-limited top tier)

### 4.1 Rarity design intent
- Common Sparks are foundational and strategically useful.
- Higher rarity increases complexity, not guaranteed superiority.
- No rarity should invalidate player skill or values.

### 4.2 Visual rarity language
- Common: simple ring glow
- Rare: dual-tone pulse
- Epic: layered pattern aura
- Legendary: signature motif animation
- Mythic: environmental reaction effect
- Ascendant: seasonal cosmic signature + title frame

---

## 5. Evolution System
Sparks evolve through Resonance Growth, not simple level grind.

### 5.1 Evolution triggers
A Spark can evolve by completing a mixed requirement profile:
- Usage milestones
- Virtue behavior milestones
- Region attunement milestones
- Guardian compatibility milestones

### 5.2 Evolution stages
- Stage I: Seed Spark
- Stage II: Active Spark
- Stage III: Signature Spark
- Stage IV: Resonant Spark
- Stage V: Apex Spark

### 5.3 Evolution philosophy
Evolution must change three layers:
- Visual identity
- Mechanical behavior
- Lore meaning

---

## 6. Acquisition Model
Sparks are obtained through gameplay identity, not pure random luck.

### 6.1 Acquisition channels
- Resonance Encounters in regions
- Dojo Trials
- Story milestones
- Tournament reward lines
- Seasonal events
- Cooperative challenges

### 6.2 Fairness model
- Every player can build a powerful roster without spending.
- High rarity acquisition includes pity and progress carry-over.
- Event exclusives return in legacy cycles.

### 6.3 Discovery fantasy
The system must create playground conversation:
- Which Spark found you?
- Where did you awaken it?
- What did it evolve into?

---

## 7. How Sparks Help Guardians
Sparks grant support modules called Spark Signatures.

### 7.1 Signature types
- Tempo Signatures: speed/rhythm adjustments
- Core Signatures: defense/offense balance shifts
- Utility Signatures: utility and tactical response
- Harmony Signatures: team synergy effects
- Mastery Signatures: advanced high-skill modifiers

### 7.2 Design rule
Sparks should alter decision-making, not auto-win outcomes.

### 7.3 Team identity
Different Spark combinations create distinct identities for the same Guardian.
This is the core of long-term personalization.

---

## 8. Regions Where Sparks Appear
Sparks are tied to Biome Resonance Belts.

Initial region model:
- Ember Belt (heat, pressure, courage)
- Tidal Belt (flow, calm, adaptation)
- Root Belt (endurance, balance, structure)
- Gale Belt (speed, creativity, freedom)
- Storm Belt (impulse, precision, determination)
- Veil Belt (focus, mystery, self-control)
- Dawn Belt (hope, support, leadership)
- Astral Belt (purpose, destiny, synthesis)

### 8.1 Regional distribution rules
- Each region has native Sparks and migrating Sparks.
- Some Sparks shift appearance by region.
- Apex forms may require cross-region attunement.

## 11. Expected Visual Design Language
Sparks must be visually distinct from Guardians.

### 11.1 Core visual principles
- Smaller scale silhouettes
- Strong iconic read in under 1 second
- Element and personality visible at first glance
- Shape language aligned with Union Guardians style

### 11.2 Detail hierarchy
- Primary read: silhouette and element
- Secondary read: core motif and behavior
- Tertiary read: texture and micro effects

### 11.3 Animation expectation
- Idle loop personality
- Signature activation loop
- Evolution transition sequence
- Discovery reveal moment

### 11.4 Visual constraints
- No direct mimic of famous creature silhouettes from external IPs
- No overcomplicated clutter that breaks readability
- No generic blob designs without identity

---

## 12. Scalability to 500+ Creatures
This system is designed from day one for large-scale growth.

### 12.1 Taxonomy architecture
Each Spark is indexed by:
- Belt origin
- Element signature
- Virtue alignment
- Behavior class
- Evolution tree family
- Seasonal lineage

### 12.2 Content pipeline strategy
- Batch creation by Spark Families
- Shared rig clusters per morphology type
- Modular VFX libraries by signature class
- Narrative rollout by regional chapters

### 12.3 Release cadence model
- Core launch: 80 to 120 Sparks
- Year 1 expansions: +120 to +180
- Year 2 expansions: +150+
- Long-term target: 500+ Sparks with controlled quality

### 12.4 Quality guardrails
A new Spark can only be approved if it passes:
- silhouette uniqueness check
- gameplay role uniqueness check
- lore coherence check
- animation readability check
- brand identity check

---

## 13. Franchise Protection Rules
To keep Sparks globally scalable and original:
- Never design by imitation of external franchises.
- Every Spark must have a clear identity sentence.
- Every Spark family must reinforce Union values.
- Expansion must privilege quality over quantity.
- No cosmetic-only inflation without mechanical or narrative purpose.
