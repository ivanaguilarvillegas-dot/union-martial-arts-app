# UNION GUARDIANS - IGNIS CONCEPT ART
Version: 1.0
Status: Official AAA concept art specification
Owner: Creative Direction - Union Guardians
Scope: Character visual lock for IGNIS only

## Purpose
This document defines the official visual identity of IGNIS for concept art, 3D modeling, rigging, animation, VFX, and AI-assisted image generation pipelines.

The goal is visual consistency: any artist or model should be able to produce the same character with matching silhouette, personality, materials, and elemental behavior.

## Character Core Identity
- Name: IGNIS
- Element: Fire
- Role fantasy: Frontline guardian striker
- Emotional core: Disciplined courage
- Visual promise: Heroic fire presence with controlled energy
- Audience target: Readable and aspirational for ages 6 to 15+

---

## 1. General Silhouette
Primary silhouette shape:
- Upward triangle with a stable base.
- Weight concentrated in torso, forearms, and grounded legs.

Recognition rules:
- Readable as IGNIS in pure black silhouette in under 2 seconds.
- Crest + broad forearms + segmented flame-tail must remain visible in all poses.
- No pose should collapse the triangular upward intent.

Mass distribution:
- Upper body visual weight: 55%
- Lower body visual weight: 45%

Negative space:
- Clear gap between forearms and torso in combat stance.
- Tail arc must open a readable negative shape behind lower back.

---

## 2. Body Proportions
Base construction unit:
- Heroic stylized biped reptilian guardian.

Proportion guide:
- Total height: 4.3 heads
- Head: 1.0
- Neck: 0.35
- Torso: 1.35
- Leg chain: 1.6
- Shoulder width: 1.45 head widths
- Forearm thickness: 1.2x upper arm
- Foot length: 0.9 head width

Scale reference:
- In-world base height: 1.18m to 1.25m
- Medium compact guardian class

Proportion constraints:
- Do not enlarge head above 25% of total height.
- Do not thin legs below grounded combat readability.

---

## 3. Facial Expression Profile
Default expression:
- Focused, calm determination.
- Protective confidence, never sadistic aggression.

Allowed expression spectrum:
- Neutral focus
- Combat intensity
- Protective urgency
- Earned victory pride
- Controlled pain in defeat

Forbidden expression spectrum:
- Maniacal grin
- Cruel enjoyment
- Horror-monster rage face
- Childish comedic over-stretch

Face readability distance target:
- Expression intent must read at medium camera distance in under 1 second.

---

## 4. Eye Design
Eye shape:
- Almond-shaped with slight upward tilt.
- Thick upper eyelid line for determination.

Iris structure:
- Outer ring: deep ember orange
- Inner ring: bright fire yellow
- Core spark: warm off-white

Pupil design:
- Soft vertical slit, not extreme predatory slit.

Eye glow behavior:
- Idle: subtle 15-20% emissive pulse
- Attack windup: 40%
- Impact frames: 70-85%
- Victory hold: 35%

---

## 5. Head Design
Head form:
- Rounded-triangle skull with reinforced brow ridge.
- Slight muzzle projection, compact jaw.
- Crest plates rising backward to indicate forward momentum.

Surface language:
- Smooth macro forms with selective thermal ridge details.
- Keep frontal readability; avoid over-spikes.

Thermal crest:
- 3 main crest segments + 2 minor separators.
- Crest edges can hold low emissive highlights in high-intensity states.

---

## 6. Horns
Official status:
- IGNIS does not have true horns in base form.
- Uses thermal crest plates that may be mistaken for horn structures.

Design constraint:
- Never add bull, ram, dragon, or demonic horn silhouettes.
- Crest must remain plate-like, ceremonial, and integrated.

---

## 7. Armor System
Armor fantasy:
- Ceremonial combat armor, not military heavy armor.

Core armor pieces:
- Shoulder guards: compact layered heat-resistant plates
- Forearm guards: segmented impact gauntlet-shells
- Chest harness: central ignis core frame
- Knee guards: light thermal protection
- Back node: ring-anchor for aura control geometry

Armor behavior goals:
- Reinforce role as disciplined protector.
- Preserve flexibility for dynamic animation.

Armor detail budget:
- Medium detail at base stage.
- Clear primary, secondary, tertiary read hierarchy.

---

## 8. Cloth Elements
Cloth use level:
- Minimal but meaningful.

Required cloth elements:
- One short ceremonial arm band or sash segment.
- One compact belt wrap accent around waist frame.

Cloth behavior:
- Fire-safe fiber look.
- Limited flutter to avoid visual noise.

Forbidden cloth patterns:
- Long capes
- Oversized scarves
- Layered robes hiding silhouette

---

## 9. Materials
Material stack:
- Skin-scales: semi-matte volcanic-organic material
- Armor plates: brushed heat-forged ceremonial alloy
- Core crystal: emissive pyro-crystal lens
- Cloth: woven flame-resistant fabric
- Claws and hard edges: dark ceramic-metal blend

PBR guidance:
- Skin roughness: medium-high
- Armor roughness: medium-low
- Emissive only on core zones, eyes, tail flame, small edge channels

Material realism target:
- Stylized AAA realism, not photoreal, not toy-like plastic.

---

## 10. Gloves and Hand Gear
Hand design:
- Integrated armored gauntlet-forearm system.
- 4 primary fingers + compact thumb structure.

Functional read:
- Hands must support guard pose, impact pose, and protective gesture.

Glove constraints:
- No boxing glove bubble shapes.
- No excessive claw length.
- Keep dexterity visible.

---

## 11. Boots and Foot Design
Footwear status:
- Organic armored feet, not separate boots.

Visual boot language:
- Layered heat-shield plating on top of feet and ankles.
- Heavy grounded sole read for impact and traction.

Locomotion requirement:
- Foot silhouette must remain stable in walk/run cycles.
- Contact frames must read weight and discipline.

---

## 12. Emblem
Primary emblem:
- Union flame sigil integrated at chest core assembly.

Secondary emblem placement:
- Optional shoulder inset mark for team/cadet variants.

Emblem behavior:
- Low glow in idle
- Pulse sync during special abilities

Design constraints:
- Emblem must stay geometric, clean, and reproducible at small scale.

---

## 13. Official Color Palette (HEX)
Primary body tones:
- Carbon Red Base: #C62828
- Structural Dark Red: #8E1B1B

Secondary active tones:
- Active Orange: #F57C00
- Secondary Orange: #FF9800

Energy tones:
- Fire Gold: #FFC107
- Ember Yellow: #FFD54F
- Warm Emissive White: #FFF3E0

Shadow/support tones:
- Ash Black: #212121
- Carbon Brown: #4E342E
- Ceremonial Bronze: #B87333

Color ratio rules:
- 60-65% body base tones
- 20-25% secondary active tones
- 10-15% emissive and metallic accents

Forbidden color use:
- Cold neon blue as dominant accent
- Purple fire gradients in base form
- Fully desaturated gray body pass

---

## 14. Lighting Direction
Lighting setup standard:
- Key light: warm 3/4 frontal top
- Fill light: soft neutral low intensity
- Rim light: controlled red-orange edge

Exposure behavior:
- Preserve core shape and armor readability.
- Never clip emissive to flat white blocks.

Cinematic variant:
- Add low-intensity environmental bounce from below for molten aura scenes.

---

## 15. Shadows
Shadow style:
- Stylized clean shadow groups with controlled softness.

Requirements:
- Contact shadow under feet always present.
- Facial shadow must preserve eye readability.
- Armor overlap shadows should clarify layer depth.

Forbidden shadow style:
- Muddy low-contrast pass
- Hyper-hard noir shadows that kill color identity

---

## 16. Fire Effects
Fire philosophy:
- Directed, disciplined flame.
- Never random wildfire chaos.

Core fire zones:
- Tail flame segment
- Core chest vent channels
- Forearm impact traces
- Eye micro-bloom during combat

Intensity presets:
- Idle: 20%
- Walk: 35%
- Combat neutral: 50%
- Attack impact: 90%
- Victory: 55%
- Defeat: 10-15% unstable but alive

---

## 17. Particle System
Particle types:
- Micro embers
- Short spark arcs
- Warm smoke wisps
- Heat distortion shimmer

Density rules:
- Keep particle count low-medium in idle.
- Burst density only at impact moments.

Color rules:
- Embers: orange-gold
- Sparks: yellow-white
- Smoke: warm dark gray with orange tint

---

## 18. Official Posture
Default posture:
- Guard-ready, chest open, center of gravity low-mid.
- Lead shoulder slightly forward.
- Tail balancing opposite combat line.

Posture emotion:
- Ready to protect first, strike second.

Do not posture as:
- Animal crouch predator
- Monster hunchback
- Comic hero chest-overinflation

---

## 19. Front View Specification
Front view goals:
- Clear symmetry in base anatomy, controlled asymmetry in accessories.
- Eye line and chest emblem centered.
- Forearm armor profile readable without pose distortion.

Front checklist:
- Head silhouette clear
- Crest segmentation visible
- Chest core emblem clean
- Leg stance balanced

---

## 20. Side View Specification
Side view goals:
- Demonstrate forward momentum line.
- Reveal crest sweep and tail trajectory.
- Show layered armor depth at shoulder and forearm.

Side checklist:
- Neck-to-torso transition clean
- Back curve supports combat movement
- Foot contact geometry grounded

---

## 21. Back View Specification
Back view goals:
- Display dorsal thermal crest logic.
- Show aura anchor ring and spine line.
- Preserve identity without face information.

Back checklist:
- Shoulder rhythm readable
- Tail base integration correct
- Back armor not cluttered

---

## 22. Combat Pose Specification
Pose intent:
- Opening pressure with defensive responsibility.

Pose structure:
- Front leg planted
- Rear leg loaded
- Lead forearm high guard
- Off-hand ready for pulse strike
- Tail arc counterbalance

VFX note:
- Forearm flame should frame the strike line, not hide anatomy.

---

## 23. Victory Pose Specification
Pose intent:
- Dignified success, no arrogance.

Pose structure:
- Upright chest
- Controlled fist-to-core oath gesture
- Soft upward flame spiral
- Focused gaze toward horizon/team

Timing note:
- Should freeze cleanly for badge cards and splash art.

---

## 24. Idle Pose Specification
Pose intent:
- Calm power under control.

Pose structure:
- Stable leg spacing
- Micro breathing cycle in chest
- Small shoulder correction every loop
- Tail low-amplitude wave

Loop behavior:
- 4.5s to 6.0s seamless loop target

---

## 25. Expected Detail Level
Target quality:
- AAA stylized production concept

Detail hierarchy:
- Primary read: silhouette and role
- Secondary read: armor layering and material contrast
- Tertiary read: emblem, vents, thermal channels

Camera use target:
- Must survive close-up hero shots and mid-distance gameplay readability.

---

## 26. Artistic Style Lock
Style statement:
- Stylized heroic fantasy with martial discipline.
- Bright but grounded color control.
- Emotional readability first.

Style blend priorities:
- 40% shape readability
- 30% material/lighting quality
- 20% character acting through pose
- 10% decorative detail

---

## 27. What Must Not Be Done
Identity protection rules:
- Do not redesign IGNIS as generic dragon, demon, or animal mascot.
- Do not add oversized horns or wings.
- Do not over-saturate all surfaces equally.
- Do not replace disciplined fire with chaotic explosion visuals.
- Do not remove ceremonial guardian cues.
- Do not push cartoon proportions beyond heroic readability.
- Do not use gritty horror textures.
- Do not mimic iconic silhouettes from external franchises.

Pipeline safety rules:
- Any concept that breaks silhouette lock must be rejected.
- Any palette outside approved HEX set requires creative director approval.
- Any armor variant must preserve chest emblem readability.

---

## 28. Prompt-Oriented Summary for AI and External Artists
Use this summary when briefing external teams:
- IGNIS is a stylized AAA fire guardian with disciplined courage.
- Biped reptilian heroic anatomy, upward triangular silhouette, broad forearms, stable legs, segmented flame-tail.
- No horns; uses thermal crest plates.
- Ceremonial armor, minimal cloth, chest fire emblem.
- Official fire palette from carbon red to ember gold with warm emissive highlights.
- Directed controlled fire effects, not chaotic wildfire.
- Expressions are determined, noble, protective.
- Must include clean front, side, and back readability plus combat, idle, and victory pose states.
